# Eric Manchester — Voice & Framing Guide

## Core Identity Statement
**"I take pipe dreams and prove they can be done"** — Focus on translating ambitious technical visions into scalable, market-ready reality.

## Authentic Voice Patterns (Extracted from Source Documents)

### How Eric Describes His Work
- **Technical precision** — Specific technologies, architectures, standards (never vague)
- **Strategic scope** — Always connects technical decisions to business outcomes
- **Systems thinking** — Emphasizes how parts integrate (e.g., "from content intake to global delivery")
- **Hands-on leadership** — "Designed and..." not "oversaw" or "managed"
- **Problem-solving orientation** — Identifies gaps before they become crises

### Achievement Framing
- Starts with **business context** (what problem needed solving)
- Moves to **technical approach** (what architecture/tool choice)
- Ends with **measurable impact** (revenue, scale, quality improvement)
- Example from master data: "Recognized accelerating OTT adoption; built prototypes that informed TWCTV Application, shaping its architecture for DRM and dynamic ad insertion..."

### Core Competencies Eric Emphasizes
- **Full system ownership** — "Independently designed, built, and maintained every component"
- **Cross-functional alignment** — Bridges engineers, executives, and clients
- **Anticipating industry shifts** — Proactive positioning, not reactive fixes
- **Scalability & reliability** — Always mentions serving millions or handling enterprise demands
- **Technical evangelism** — Teaching others, making complex topics accessible

### What Eric Does NOT Say
- "Helped" (use "built", "architected", "designed", "delivered")
- Vague collaboration terms without specifying contribution
- Apologies or minimizations ("tried to", "attempted")
- Jargon without business context
- Personal motivations ("passionate about", "love building")

## Tone by Role Type

### For Solutions Architect / Pre-Sales Roles
- **Tone:** Strategic consultant, customer-focused architect
- **Emphasis:** Business fit, scalability, vendor evaluation, customer outcomes
- **Pattern:** "Designed end-to-end solutions spanning X platforms for Y use cases, resulting in Z"
- **Example from master:** "Translate complex customer requirements into scalable solution architectures"

### For Principal Engineer / Technical Leadership Roles
- **Tone:** Technical authority, systems thinker, mentor
- **Emphasis:** Technical decisions, architecture patterns, team enablement
- **Pattern:** "Architected and implemented X using Y technologies, achieving Z scalability"
- **Example from master:** "Lead technical solutions teams in delivering complex products"

### For Product / AI-Focused Roles
- **Tone:** Market-aware technologist, user-focused
- **Emphasis:** Market trends, user needs, product-market fit, AI/ML applied to real problems
- **Pattern:** "Anticipated market need for X; delivered Y product that achieved Z results"
- **Example from master:** "Anticipated growing audience demand for interactive sports; led architecture of Key Plays..."

## Key Phrases Eric Uses Authentically

From master data patterns:
- "Independently designed..."
- "Operated autonomously..."
- "Anticipated [trend/need]..."
- "End-to-end [system/pipeline/delivery]..."
- "From [start point] to [end point]..."
- "Serving millions of users..."
- "Large-scale..."
- "Cloud-native..."
- "Media-grade architecture and security..."

## What Makes Eric's Voice Credible

1. **Specificity over superlatives** — Uses real numbers, specific technologies, measurable outcomes
2. **System-level thinking** — Never isolated features, always the full ecosystem
3. **Execution focus** — "Built", "designed", "delivered" not "led" or "managed" without proof
4. **Industry context** — References standards (SCTE, SMPTE, HLS, DASH) naturally, not as buzzwords
5. **Mentor/teacher orientation** — Explains how he enabled others, not how many people reported to him

## Red Flags (Non-Authentic)
- ❌ "Pioneering" (use "early adopter" or specific achievement)
- ❌ "Team of X people" (specify actual contribution, not headcount)
- ❌ "Drove" without outcome (use "designed", "architected", "delivered" with results)
- ❌ Empty modifiers ("innovative", "dynamic", "cutting-edge")
- ❌ Clichés ("passion", "synergy", "holistic")

## Summary
Eric's authentic voice is: **Direct, specific, outcome-focused, systems-thinking, technically precise, and unapologetically ambitious.** Never corporate-polished or soft-spoken. The writing should sound like someone who asks better questions and builds better systems than the people around him.
