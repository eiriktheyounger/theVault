# AOL — Senior Live Events Producer (2001–2006)

## Role Summary

Pioneering senior leader in AOL's broadcast operations center (BOC), responsible for designing and building the first all-digital downlink facility for streaming/OTT content. Directed operations for 24/7 live and on-demand video/radio channels while managing the overall vendor lifecycle. Directly led Emmy Award-winning Live 8 Concert streaming—a world record for concurrent live event coverage.

## Key Responsibilities

- Identified that AOL's existing Broadcast Operations Center could not meet projected growth in streaming; secured multi-million-dollar budget and executive approval to design, build, and lead a new facility
- Leveraged the new BOC to deliver global coverage of the Live 8 Concerts from eight sites, setting a world record for live streaming
- Directed operations for 24/7 live and on-demand video/radio channels, aligning output with rapidly growing consumer appetite for online media
- Created the first all-digital downlink broadcast operations center specifically for Streaming/OTT content
- Managed the broadcast operations center and the attached 100 channel radio / 34 channel video and dedicated streaming studio
- Directed acquisition, transcoding, and distribution for the Emmy Award-winning Live 8 Concert Event
- Led production of 24x7 linear feeds for news events and managed the integration of a multi-camera production studio
- Managed the full vendor lifecycle, including finding vendors, negotiating contracts, managing the entire architecture design through build-out, and handover

## Key Accomplishments

### Accomplishment 1: AOL Broadcast Operations Center Design & Build

**Situation**: AOL's existing BOC was unstable, low-capacity, and under-equipped for growing live event coverage.

**Task**: Lead a ground-up replacement of the AOL BOC: budget, architecture, vendor selection, build, and operations.

**Action**:
- Secured multi-million-dollar budget and defined technical requirements
- Designed facility with 100+ audio channels and 34+ video channels, supported by master control override
- Built redundancy, monitoring, and a kids-focused live/audio/studio streaming solution
- Managed vendor relationships, negotiated contracts, and oversaw full implementation
- Delivered runbooks, trained operations, and provided day-one launch support

**Result**:
- AOL's first fully digital downlink facility for live streaming
- Successfully supported all Live 8 concert streams—recognized with an **Emmy award**
- Platform became the foundation for large-scale live event operations across AOL properties

---

### Accomplishment 2: CNN Patent — Early Ad Targeting Engine

**Situation**: CNN approached AOL with a need: enable targeted advertising in live streaming at a time when no real ad tech systems existed.

**Task**: Develop a targeting solution that allowed per-user variation in ads and content using existing CDN infrastructure.

**Action**:
- Formed diverse ideation group across engineering, ad ops, and streaming teams
- Proposed a **weight-based targeting model**—users and content assigned numeric traits
- Designed logic for real-time decisioning at CDN layer—dynamic stream switching
- Applied same logic to Shoutcast for Pandora-like music transitions
- Generalized system into reusable targeting engine
- Led patent process through AOL IP team with full attribution to contributors

**Result**:
- **Patent granted**: early ad targeting logic using attribute weighting across streams
- System used by AOL and **acquired later by Facebook** for underlying IP
- Created personalization framework years ahead of mainstream DSPs
- Technology used across ad, music, and user-specific content routing

---

### Accomplishment 3: NBA Ops Mistake & Recovery

**Situation**: AOL's BOC streams regularly missed NBA pregame commentary due to late starts.

**Task**: Identify root cause and implement solution to ensure BOC/NOC team reliability for live starts.

**Action**:
- Monitored event launches in real-time, discovered operational delays from unclear delegation
- Identified NOC staff lacked understanding of live broadcast timing
- Developed detailed runbooks and training program for managers and team
- Personally covered shifts until process stability was confirmed

**Result**:
- Resolved start-time issues, dramatically reducing missed pregame content
- Created repeatable training and operations protocol
- Influenced future ops design for TWC TV and VOD streaming operations
