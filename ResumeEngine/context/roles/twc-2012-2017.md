# Time Warner Cable — Principal Engineer, Video-On-Demand (2012–2017)

## Role Summary

Principal architect responsible for TWCTV application development and VOD platform strategy. Recognized the accelerating shift to OTT adoption and built prototypes that informed TWCTV's architecture for DRM and dynamic ad insertion. Designed and directed centralization of all VOD/ad content delivery across regions. Operated as independent architect determining technical direction and securing executive buy-in for major platform changes. Emmy Award winner for TWCTV Application innovation.

## Key Responsibilities

- Recognized accelerating OTT adoption; built prototypes that informed the TWCTV Application, shaping its architecture for DRM and dynamic ad insertion to meet emerging security and monetization needs
- Directed creation of a centralized headend for all VOD/ad content, unifying workflows across regions to improve scalability and reduce operational costs
- Operated as an independent architect, determining technical direction and securing executive buy-in for major platform changes
- Acted as SME for groups from engineers to C-Suites on support content acquisition, processing, CDN based distribution and user playback for new and innovative streaming content solutions
- Designed and drove centralization of all VOD content acquisition and distribution for the Set Top Box environment
- Key Lead on the team creating the TWCTV application, focusing on VOD and advertising areas
- Directed the implementation of just-in-time encryption DRM solutions, improving security and scalability for VOD
- Evaluated, partnered, and helped develop hardware and software that best solved complex problems, often managing multiple hardware, software, and SaaS providers simultaneously
- Led team of 4-6 people utilizing Agile methodologies to align and assign tasks
