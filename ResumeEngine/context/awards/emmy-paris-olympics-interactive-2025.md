# Outstanding Interactive Experience Emmy Award
## 46th Annual Sports Emmy Awards, May 20, 2025
## Paris 2024 Summer Olympics, NBC Sports / Peacock

### Award Details
**Official Name:** Outstanding Interactive Experience
**Ceremony:** 46th Annual Sports Emmy Awards
**Date:** May 20, 2025
**Recipient Organization:** NBC Sports / Peacock — The Games of the XXXIII Olympiad (Paris 2024)
**Eric's Credit:** Individual named recipient as **Software Developer** (sole person in this role category)
**Status:** VERIFIED — sourced from NBCU press releases, Sports Video Group, Sports Business Journal

### Achievement Summary
Emmy Award recognizing groundbreaking interactive viewing experience during Paris 2024 Olympics that integrated AI-driven personalization, fan-controlled viewing journeys, and real-time interactive tools to manage unprecedented live event volumes. Eric Manchester individually credited as Software Developer for architecting and building core interactive features.

### Eric's Specific Contributions

#### Live Actions — Architected & Built from Ground Up
- **Scope:** End-to-end product development from concept through production
- **Functionality:** Interactive tool enabling fans to choose their own viewing journey, follow specific athletes or events in real-time
- **Patent:** Technology underlying Live Actions produced patent-pending **Timed Metadata for Overlays (US-20250203152-A1)**
- **Role:** Primary architect and builder

#### Olympic Daily Recap — Workflows & Architecture
- **Scope:** Content workflow architecture for AI-powered personalized highlight playlist
- **Functionality:** Generated 7M+ personalized video variants featuring Al Michaels' AI-synthesized voice narration
- **Scale:** System generated millions of individual recap variations personalized to viewer preferences
- **Role:** Architect of full content pipelines and workflows (did not build underlying AI voice platform)
- **Impact:** Key driver of viewer engagement during Games

#### Distinction: Individual vs. Team Contributions
- **Eric Built:** Live Actions product (end-to-end), Daily Recap content workflows and architecture
- **Team/Organization Built:** AI voice synthesis platform, Discovery Multiview, broader platform infrastructure
- **Individual Credit:** Eric is named on Emmy as sole Software Developer credited — eligible for Emmy statuette

### Scale & Impact (Verified Numbers)

#### Coverage & Programming
- **Events:** All 329 medal events covered
- **Live Programming:** 5,000+ hours
- **Platforms:** Peacock (primary), NBCOlympics.com

#### Viewership & Streaming Performance
- **Total Streaming:** 23.5 billion minutes
- **Growth vs. Prior Olympics:** 40% increase over ALL combined prior Summer and Winter Olympics streaming
- **Total Audience Delivery:** Averaging 30.6 million viewers
- **Year-Over-Year Growth:** 82% increase in viewership vs. Tokyo 2020 Games
- **Platform Record:** All-time streaming record for Peacock platform

#### Interactive Engagement
- **Personalized Variants Generated:** 7M+ unique Olympic Daily Recap videos
- **AI Voice Narration:** Al Michaels' synthesized voice across all variants (SAG-AFTRA compliant)
- **Fan-Driven Viewing:** Millions of fans used Live Actions to customize their Olympic experience

### Interactive Experience Elements

#### Complete Emmy Submission Scope
1. **Peacock Live Actions** (Eric architected & built) — Fan-driven viewing journey enabling individual athlete/event selection
2. **Discovery Multiview** (team-built) — Four-view simultaneous experience with real-time descriptions and stakes information
3. **Your Daily Olympic Recap** (Eric architected workflows) — AI-powered personalized highlight playlist with synthesized narration
4. **Interactive Schedule & Medal Standings** — Real-time navigation, results tracking, and medal updates

#### Viewer Experience
- Viewers could customize viewing journey in real-time
- Switch between events, athletes, or sports on demand
- Receive personalized recap highlights aligned with preferences
- Access real-time medal standings and event scheduling
