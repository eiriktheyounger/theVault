# CNN Ad Targeting — Weight-Based Personalization System

## Project Summary

Pioneering ad targeting system enabling per-user variation in ads and content during live streaming, at a time when no real ad tech or DSP systems existed. Invented weight-based targeting model using numeric traits assigned to users and content. Applied same logic across CNN streaming, Shoutcast audio platform, and generalized into reusable targeting engine. Technology later acquired by Facebook for underlying IP.

## Background & Challenge

CNN approached AOL with a need: enable targeted advertising in live streaming—at a time when no real ad tech or DSP systems existed. Had to invent a solution from scratch with limited tools, teams, and technology.

## Key Contribution

**Solution Architect & Patent Contributor**: Developed weight-based targeting concept, designed CDN-layer decisioning, and led patent process.

## Key Actions

- Formed diverse ideation group across engineering, ad ops, and streaming teams
- Proposed **weight-based targeting model**—users + content assigned numeric traits
- Designed logic for real-time decisioning at CDN layer—dynamic stream switching
- Applied same logic to Shoutcast: enabled Pandora-like music transitions by user preference
- Generalized system into reusable targeting engine for future experiences
- Led patent process through AOL IP team → ensured full attribution to all contributors
- Declined bonus maximization in favor of team credit when legal asked about payout impact

## Key Results

- **Patent granted**: early ad targeting logic using attribute weighting across streams (US-8028092)
- System used by AOL and **acquired later by Facebook** for underlying IP
- Created **personalization framework years ahead of mainstream DSPs**
- Used across ad, music, and user-specific content routing
- Built loyalty and trust among team through shared credit and co-ownership
