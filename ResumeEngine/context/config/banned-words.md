# Resume Language — Banned Words List

Comprehensive list of words and phrases to exclude from all resume and professional communications. These terms are overused, clichéd, or undermine credibility.

## Banned Terms

### Marketing Clichés
- **spearheaded** — Overused action verb; replace with specific role (architected, designed, built, led)
- **leveraged** — Weak passive construction; replace with active verb (used, integrated, applied)
- **revolutionized** — Hyperbole; replace with specific achievement (transformed, modernized, innovated)
- **synergy** — Corporate jargon; remove or specify actual collaboration
- **impactful** — Vague; specify the actual impact with metrics
- **best-in-class** — Unsubstantiated claim; use specific metrics instead
- **thought leader** — Vague positioning; substantiate with specific achievements
- **passionate about** — Overused and weak; demonstrate passion through accomplishments
- **results-driven** — Cliché; show results instead of stating intent
- **dynamic professional** — Meaningless filler; remove entirely

### Alternative Actions
Instead of banned terms, use specific, active verbs:
- **spearheaded** → architected, designed, built, directed, led, founded, created, launched
- **leveraged** → integrated, utilized, implemented, deployed, applied, harnessed
- **revolutionized** → transformed, modernized, reimagined, optimized, advanced, pioneered

### Context-Specific Guidance

#### Describing Innovation
- ❌ "Revolutionized streaming technology"
- ✓ "Pioneered world-record concurrent live streaming (5M+ simultaneous viewers, 2005)"
- ✓ "Architected personalization system generating 7M+ video variants"

#### Describing Leadership
- ❌ "Passionate about mentoring and developing talent"
- ✓ "Mentored junior TPM to lead role; individual became principal project lead"
- ❌ "Dynamic team leader"
- ✓ "Led cross-functional teams through platform transitions; drove technical decisions as principal architect"

#### Describing Achievement
- ❌ "Impactful revenue optimization"
- ✓ "Increased ad revenue 350% through VOD automation"
- ✓ "Generated 200%+ incremental ad revenue through interactive features"

#### Describing Scope
- ❌ "Best-in-class platform"
- ✓ "Served millions of concurrent users across Peacock, Sky Showtime, Showmax"
- ✓ "All-time streaming records for platform (23.5 billion minutes)"

## Implementation Rules

1. **Search and Replace:** Before finalizing any resume, search for all banned terms above and replace with specific, active language
2. **Metric Priority:** When removing vague terms, always replace with quantified accomplishment (numbers, scale, scope)
3. **Action Verification:** Ensure every bullet uses a strong, specific action verb
4. **Specificity Check:** Ask "Could someone else claim this same statement?" If yes, make it more specific

## Why These Words Are Banned

- **Overused:** Every resume uses these terms, making them background noise
- **Vague:** They don't convey specific accomplishment or scope
- **Weak:** They rely on adjectives instead of substantive facts
- **Unsubstantiated:** They assert value without proof
- **Passive:** Some mask the actual action behind corporate jargon

## Voice Alignment

These banned terms conflict with Eric's established voice:
- Specific and factual (not hyperbolic)
- Metric-driven (not adjective-driven)
- Technical depth (not marketing speak)
- Direct and clear (not corporate jargon)

---

**Note:** This list applies to all resume formats (technical, product, leadership, brief, detailed). Consistency across all materials reinforces professional brand.
