# Framing Rules — Context-Specific Guidance

Strategic framing rules for positioning accomplishments and employment history across different resume contexts and opportunities.

## NBCUniversal Departure (Critical Rule)

### The Rule
**Frame departure as "organizational restructuring" only. Max 2 sentences. Do not mention under normal circumstances.**

**Exact Language:**
- "NBCUniversal underwent organizational restructuring in early 2025."
- "Left NBCUniversal following organizational restructuring in February 2025."

### Why This Rule Exists
- Clean professional narrative without speculation about internal politics
- Focuses forward on current work (Harmonic) instead of explaining past change
- Avoids negative framing while remaining truthful
- Industry standard for discussing internal reorganizations

### When to Mention
- ✓ When explicitly asked about employment gap
- ✓ When explaining timeline between positions
- ✓ In response to "Why did you leave NBCUniversal?"
- ✓ In LinkedIn employment history (dates may be visible)

### When NOT to Mention
- ✓ Don't volunteer this on application materials
- ✓ Don't include in resume unless gap requires explanation
- ✓ Don't discuss in cover letters unless employment history explains gap
- ✓ Don't mention in summary or objective sections

### What NOT to Say
- ❌ Details about restructuring scope or impact
- ❌ Commentary on restructuring decisions
- ❌ Speculation about causes or timing
- ❌ Comparison to other restructuring eras
- ❌ Emotional language about the transition

### Amplify Instead
After briefly mentioning departure, immediately pivot to positive:
- "Since departing NBCUniversal, I've focused on [current work at Harmonic]"
- "I'm now applying that interactive platform expertise at Harmonic as Solutions Architect"
- "Currently architecting cloud-native broadcast solutions at Harmonic"

## Achievement Framing

### Revenue Impact
- **Frame as:** "Increased ad revenue [X%] through [specific technology/feature]"
- **Example:** "Increased ad revenue 350% through VOD automation" (TWC)
- **Example:** "Generated 200%+ incremental ad revenue through interactive features" (NBCU)

### Scale and Impact
- **Frame as:** Specific user scale, concurrent viewers, or content processed
- **Examples:**
  - "Served millions of concurrent users"
  - "23.5 billion streaming minutes"
  - "7M+ personalized video variants"
  - "5M+ concurrent viewers" (Live 8)

### Technical Innovation
- **Frame as:** Specific technology, patent, or architectural innovation
- **Examples:**
  - "Architected and built Peacock Live Actions from ground up"
  - "Designed overlay platform enabling real-time interactive experiences"
  - "Pioneered asynchronous workflow orchestration for broadcast-scale content processing"

### Award Recognition
- **Frame as:** Specific Emmy category and year
- **Examples:**
  - "Technology & Engineering Emmy (2023) for Key Plays AI/ML feature"
  - "Outstanding Interactive Experience Emmy (2025) for Paris 2024 Olympics"
  - "Individually named as sole Software Developer on Emmy-winning interactive experience"

## Scope Framing

### Individual Contributions
When you were sole contributor or principal architect:
- "I architected..." (not "I was part of a team that...")
- "I designed..." (not "I contributed to...")
- "I built..." (not "I helped build...")
- "I led..." (not "I was involved in...")

### Team Contributions
When clarifying team work while emphasizing your role:
- "I designed [component]; team built [other component]"
- "I architected the system; engineering team executed implementation"
- "I led technical decisions for [scope]; team executed across [broader scope]"

### Example: Paris Olympics Emmy
- ✓ "I architected and built Peacock Live Actions from the ground up"
- ✓ "I designed content workflows for Olympic Daily Recap (7M+ variants)"
- ✓ "Individually named as sole Software Developer on Emmy award"
- ✗ "I was part of the team that won an Emmy"
- ✗ "I built the AI voice platform" (team effort, not Eric's work)
- ✗ "I built Discovery Multiview" (team-built feature)

## Timeline and Context Framing

### Overlapping Roles
RE Kreations (founder/side project 2017-present):
- Frame as: "Concurrent innovation during full-time roles at [company]"
- Emphasize: Autonomous projects, emerging technology research
- Don't overstate: Keep focus on primary employment

### Gaps or Transitions
- Always explain gaps briefly (2-3 sentences max)
- Focus forward on what you gained or what you're doing now
- Avoid negative framing of any transition

### Concurrent Companies (AOL era work at Facebook)
- If both appear on resume: Clarify that patents filed during primary employment
- Frame as: "Concurrent innovation" or "Parallel technical leadership"

## What NOT to Frame

### Avoid These Framings
- ❌ "Best company I worked at" — Just don't say it
- ❌ "Perfect role for my skills" — Comes across as insincere
- ❌ "Dream job" — Overused; show through specifics instead
- ❌ "Team dynamics challenges" — Never volunteer negative context
- ❌ Speculation about other companies' decisions
- ❌ Complaints about tools, processes, or people

### Positive Pivot
If asked about challenges, frame as growth:
- "I learned [skill/lesson] that strengthened [capability]"
- "That experience clarified my interest in [direction]"
- "Working with [constraint] deepened my expertise in [area]"

## Application Type Framing

### Technical Role Application
Emphasize: Technical depth, architecture decisions, innovation
- Highlight: Patents, Emmy Awards, specific technologies
- Example: "Architected Live Actions overlay platform from ground up using frame-accurate metadata synchronization (US-20250203152)"

### Product/Leadership Role Application
Emphasize: Strategy, team impact, business outcomes
- Highlight: Revenue growth, user scale, team mentorship
- Example: "Led Peacock interactive and advertising strategy as Principal Architect; drove 200%+ ad revenue growth through features like pause ads and Live Actions"

### Executive/Director Role Application
Emphasize: Strategic vision, organizational impact, industry positioning
- Highlight: Emmy Awards, patent portfolio, market leadership
- Example: "Principal Architect driving Peacock's interactive personalization strategy; 4-time Emmy Award winner for technical innovation; patent holder across streaming, advertising, and AI/ML domains"
